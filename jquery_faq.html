10 FAQS about National Parks

Wrangell National Park

  • This park is a beautiful place to go and explore!!!
  • Wrangell-St. Elias National Park is an 8,323,147 acre park located in Alaska.
  • Wrangell-St. Elias National Park is larger than the entire country of Switzerland.
  • Between 1903 and 1938 the park's center - a boomtown called Kennecott - exploded because of the areas rich copper deposit discovery

Yellow Stone National Park

  • This park is a beautiful place to go and explore!!!
  • Yellowstone was established on March 1, 1872.
  • Yellowstone has more than 290 waterfalls.
  • Yellowstone was the world's first national park

Big Bend National Park

  • This park is a beautiful place to go and explore!!!
  • Big Bend National Park is considered to be the largest Chihuahuan Desert protected region in the country.
  • fBig Bend National Park is home to more than 1200 plant species, including the desert marigold, pink bluebonnets, ocotillo, lechuguilla, and rock nettle
  • angford Hot Springs are located in Big Bend National Park. The temperature of the spring is approximately 105 degrees Fahrenheit.

Sequoia National Park

  • This park is a beautiful place to go and explore!!!
  • Sequoia National Park was the second national park established in the United States - in 1890. It was established to protect the region's giant trees from logging.
  • Sequoia trees are naturally fire resistant. They have thick bark that can often protect them from burning down. If they do burn their nutrients are deposited directly into the ground to feed other trees.
  • 84% of Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks is designated as wilderness. Most of the parks land is not accessible by roadway.
